# Geological Assessment

## Purpose

Evaluate the geological setting of a region or project area to assess mineral prospectivity, identify exploration targets, and understand the geological controls on mineralization.

## Inputs

- **Region or project location**: Country, state/province, coordinates
- **Target commodity** (if specified)
- **Available geological data**: Maps, reports, previous exploration

## Workflow

### Step 1: Regional Geology
- Identify the geological province and tectonic setting
- Describe the main rock units (age, lithology, metamorphic grade)
- Identify major structural features (faults, shear zones, fold axes)
- Assess the regional metallogenic framework

### Step 2: Deposit Model Analysis
- Identify applicable mineral deposit models for the geological setting
- Compare known deposits in the region to type-deposit characteristics
- Assess which deposit models are most prospective

### Step 3: Prospectivity Indicators
- **Lithological**: Favorable host rocks, intrusive relationships
- **Structural**: Fault intersections, dilational zones, fold hinges
- **Alteration**: Hydrothermal alteration haloes, geochemical anomalies
- **Geophysical**: Magnetic, gravity, or electromagnetic signatures (if available)
- **Geochemical**: Stream sediment, soil, or rock chip anomalies

### Step 4: Target Generation
- Rank exploration targets by prospectivity score
- Define target areas with coordinates/boundaries
- Estimate depth to target and likely deposit size range
- Recommend exploration methods (mapping, geochemistry, geophysics, drilling)

### Step 5: Risk Assessment
- Geological uncertainty (limited data, complex geology)
- Access and environmental constraints
- Tenement status and competing claims

## Data Sources

- USGS geological maps and databases
- National geological survey publications
- Published geological literature and technical reports
- Satellite imagery for structural interpretation
