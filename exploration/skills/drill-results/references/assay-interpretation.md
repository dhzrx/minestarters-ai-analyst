# Assay Interpretation Guide

## Cut-Off Grades by Commodity

| Commodity | Open Pit Cut-Off | Underground Cut-Off | Units |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------------|-------|
| Gold | 0.3–0.5 | 2.0–3.0 | g/t |
| Copper | 0.2–0.3 | 0.5–1.0 | % |
| Lithium | 0.5–1.0 | 0.8–1.5 | % Li2O |
| Nickel Sulphide | 0.3–0.5 | 0.5–1.0 | % Ni |
| Nickel Laterite | 0.8–1.0 | N/A | % Ni |
| Zinc | 1.0–2.0 | 3.0–5.0 | % Zn |
| REE (TREO) | 0.5–1.0 | 1.0–2.0 | % TREO |

## Compositing Rules

- **Maximum internal dilution**: 2m (can vary by deposit type)
- **Minimum composite length**: Typically equal to minimum mining width
- **Top-cutting**: Apply when coefficient of variation > 1.5; use log-probability plots
- **Below detection limit**: Assign half the detection limit value

## Quality Assurance / Quality Control (QAQC)

- Certified Reference Materials (standards): ±2 standard deviations
- Blanks: Below 5x detection limit
- Duplicates (field and lab): ±20% relative for most commodities
- Umpire laboratory checks: 5-10% of samples

## Common Pitfalls

1. Reporting composite intercepts without stating cut-off grade
2. Mixing true width and downhole width without clarification
3. Ignoring nugget effect in high-grade gold deposits
4. Insufficient QAQC to support reported grades
5. Not accounting for specific gravity in tonnage estimates
