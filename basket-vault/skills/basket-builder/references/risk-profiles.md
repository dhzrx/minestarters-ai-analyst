# Mining Investment Risk Profiles

## Conservative Profile
- **Target return**: 8-12% annualized
- **Max drawdown tolerance**: 15-20%
- **Volatility target**: <15% annualized
- **Characteristics**:
  - Majority producing assets with established cash flows
  - Tier 1 jurisdictions dominant
  - Large-cap, diversified miners preferred
  - Precious metals as portfolio stabilizer
  - Minimal exploration-stage exposure

## Balanced Profile
- **Target return**: 12-18% annualized
- **Max drawdown tolerance**: 25-35%
- **Volatility target**: 15-25% annualized
- **Characteristics**:
  - Mix of producing and development-stage projects
  - Tier 1 and Tier 2 jurisdictions
  - Mid-cap miners and developers
  - Commodity diversification across base, precious, and specialty
  - Moderate exploration upside allocation

## Aggressive Profile
- **Target return**: 18-30%+ annualized
- **Max drawdown tolerance**: 40-50%+
- **Volatility target**: 25-40%+ annualized
- **Characteristics**:
  - Significant exploration and early-development exposure
  - Higher concentration in individual projects
  - Emerging/frontier jurisdictions accepted
  - Single-commodity thematic bets acceptable
  - Small-cap, junior miners

## Risk Metrics Definitions

| Metric | Description | Calculation |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Sharpe Ratio | Risk-adjusted return | (Return - Risk-Free Rate) / Volatility |
| Sortino Ratio | Downside risk-adjusted return | (Return - MAR) / Downside Deviation |
| Max Drawdown | Largest peak-to-trough decline | Max(Peak - Trough) / Peak |
| Value at Risk (95%) | Max expected loss at 95% confidence | Parametric or historical VaR |
| Correlation | Co-movement with benchmarks | Pearson correlation to mining index |

## Mining-Specific Risk Factors

1. **Commodity price cyclicality**: Mining returns are highly correlated with commodity prices
2. **Operational risk**: Mine plans frequently encounter geological or engineering surprises
3. **Permitting risk**: Environmental and social approvals can delay or halt projects
4. **Sovereign risk**: Changes in mining law, royalties, or nationalization
5. **Liquidity risk**: Token markets may have limited depth
6. **Concentration risk**: Small portfolios inherently concentrated
