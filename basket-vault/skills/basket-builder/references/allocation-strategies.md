# Mining Basket Allocation Strategies

## Core-Satellite Approach
- **Core (60-70%)**: Producing mines with stable cash flows — provide income and stability
- **Satellite (30-40%)**: Development and exploration projects — provide growth upside

## Commodity Cycle Strategy
- **Early cycle**: Overweight copper, zinc, nickel (infrastructure demand)
- **Mid cycle**: Balanced across base and precious metals
- **Late cycle**: Overweight gold, silver (safe haven), reduce base metals
- **Downturn**: Concentrate in lowest-cost producers, increase cash

## Energy Transition Strategy
- **Battery metals focus**: Li (30%), Cu (25%), Ni (20%), Co (10%), Graphite (10%), Mn (5%)
- **Rationale**: EV and energy storage demand growth
- **Risk**: Technology substitution, oversupply cycles

## Precious Metals Strategy
- **Gold focus**: Au (60%), Ag (20%), PGMs (20%)
- **Rationale**: Inflation hedge, monetary demand, industrial (PGMs)
- **Risk**: Real rate sensitivity, ETF flow volatility

## Geographic Diversification Guidelines

### Tier 1 Jurisdictions (Lower Risk)
Australia, Canada, USA, Chile, Botswana, Finland, Sweden, Ireland
- Target: 50-70% of conservative baskets

### Tier 2 Jurisdictions (Moderate Risk)
Peru, Mexico, Brazil, South Africa, DRC (with mitigation), Philippines, Indonesia
- Target: 20-40% of balanced baskets

### Tier 3 Jurisdictions (Higher Risk)
Emerging/frontier mining jurisdictions
- Target: 0-20%, aggressive baskets only
- Require specific risk mitigation (political risk insurance, local partnerships)

## Rebalancing Approaches

| Approach | Frequency | Trigger | Best For |
|----------|-----------|---------|----------|
| Calendar | Quarterly | Time-based | Low turnover, tax efficiency |
| Threshold | On drift | 5% band breach | Active management, drift control |
| Tactical | Discretionary | Market events | Opportunistic, high conviction |
| Hybrid | Quarterly + drift | Both | Most baskets (recommended) |
