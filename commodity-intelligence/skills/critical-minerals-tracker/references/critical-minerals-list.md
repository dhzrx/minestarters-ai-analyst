# Critical Minerals Lists by Jurisdiction

50 minerals designated as critical:

### Group 1: Energy Storage & Batteries
Lithium, Cobalt, Nickel, Graphite (natural), Manganese, Vanadium

### Group 2: Rare Earth Elements
Cerium, Dysprosium, Erbium, Europium, Gadolinium, Holmium, Lanthanum, Lutetium, Neodymium, Praseodymium, Samarium, Scandium, Terbium, Thulium, Ytterbium, Yttrium

### Group 3: Technology Metals
Gallium, Germanium, Indium, Tellurium, Selenium, Beryllium, Bismuth, Hafnium, Niobium, Rhenium, Rubidium, Strontium, Tantalum, Titanium, Tungsten, Zirconium

### Group 4: Other Critical
Aluminum (bauxite), Antimony, Arsenic, Barite, Cesium, Chromium, Fluorspar, Iridium, Magnesium, Palladium, Platinum, Rhodium, Ruthenium, Tin, Zinc

## European Union — Critical Raw Materials Act (2024)

### Strategic Raw Materials (SRMs)
Subject to benchmarks: Bismuth, Boron, Cobalt, Copper, Gallium, Germanium, Lithium, Magnesium, Manganese, Natural Graphite, Nickel, PGMs, REE, Silicon, Titanium, Tungsten

### Critical Raw Materials (CRMs)
34 materials including all SRMs plus: Antimony, Arsenic, Barite, Bauxite, Beryllium, Feldspar, Fluorspar, Hafnium, Helium, HREEs, LREEs, Niobium, Phosphate rock, Phosphorus, Scandium, Strontium, Tantalum, Vanadium

### EU Benchmarks (by 2030)
- Extraction: ≥10% of EU annual consumption
- Processing: ≥40% of EU annual consumption
- Recycling: ≥25% of EU annual consumption
- Single-source limit: ≤65% from any one third country
