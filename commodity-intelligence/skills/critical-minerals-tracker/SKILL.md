# Critical Minerals Tracker

## Purpose

Monitor and analyze critical minerals essential to the energy transition and national security. Track supply chain developments, government policies, and investment implications across lithium, copper, cobalt, nickel, rare earths, graphite, and manganese.

## Tracked Minerals

### Tier 1: Highest Priority
| Mineral | Key Use | Supply Risk | Demand Growth |
|---------|---------|------------|---------------|
| **Lithium** | EV batteries | High (concentrated) | Very High (20-30%/yr) |
| **Copper** | Electrification | Medium (diversified) | High (3-5%/yr) |
| **Nickel** | Batteries, steel | Medium (Indonesia dominant) | High (8-12%/yr for battery) |
| **Cobalt** | Battery cathodes | Very High (DRC 70%) | Moderate (declining per kWh) |

### Tier 2: Important
| Mineral | Key Use | Supply Risk | Demand Growth |
|---------|---------|------------|---------------|
| **Rare Earths** | Magnets, wind turbines | Very High (China 60%+) | High (8-10%/yr) |
| **Graphite** | Battery anodes | High (China 65%+) | Very High (20-25%/yr) |
| **Manganese** | Batteries, steel | Medium | Moderate |

## Tracking Dimensions

### 1. Supply Chain Mapping
For each mineral, track:
- **Mine**: Top producing countries and companies, new projects
- **Processing**: Refining/conversion capacity by country (China dominance)
- **Manufacturing**: Battery/component makers and their supply agreements
- **End Use**: OEMs, energy companies, defense procurement
- **Recycling**: Current rates, planned capacity, economics

### 2. Policy Tracker
| Policy | Jurisdiction | Impact |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) | USA | Tax credits tied to domestic/FTA sourcing; critical mineral content requirements for EV credits |
| EU Critical Raw Materials Act | EU | 10% extraction, 40% processing, 25% recycling targets by 2030 |
| Australia Critical Minerals Strategy | Australia | A$2B+ financing, strategic reserve |
| Canada Critical Minerals Strategy | Canada | C$3.8B investment, permitting acceleration |
| Japan Economic Security Act | Japan | Stockpiling, supply chain diversification |
| Korea Battery Alliance | South Korea | Battery supply chain investment |
| China Export Controls | China | Gallium, germanium, graphite export restrictions |

### 3. Supply Chain Risks
| Risk | Description | Affected Minerals |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Geographic concentration | >60% from one country | Co, REE, Graphite, Ga, Ge |
| Processing bottleneck | >80% processed in China | Li, Co, REE, Graphite |
| ESG concerns | Child labor, deforestation | Co (DRC), Ni (Indonesia) |
| Trade restrictions | Export controls, tariffs | REE, Graphite (China) |
| Permitting delays | 10-15 year mine development cycle | All (Western jurisdictions) |

## Workflow

### Step 1: Market Update
- Current prices for all tracked minerals
- Recent price changes and drivers
- Notable market events (supply disruptions, policy changes)

### Step 2: Supply Chain Analysis
- Update production data and pipeline
- Track processing capacity changes
- Monitor trade flow disruptions
- Assess recycling developments

### Step 3: Policy Impact Assessment
- New policy announcements and their implications
- Subsidy/incentive changes
- Trade restriction updates
- Permitting and regulatory changes

### Step 4: Investment Implications
- Which minerals face tightest supply?
- Where are the best exploration/development opportunities?
- Which jurisdictions benefit most from policy trends?
- What are the key risks to monitor?
