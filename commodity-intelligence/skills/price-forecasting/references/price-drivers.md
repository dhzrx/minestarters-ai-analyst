# Commodity Price Drivers

## Gold (Au)
### Primary Drivers
- **US real interest rates** (inverse correlation): Higher real rates → lower gold
- **US dollar strength** (inverse): Stronger USD → lower gold
- **Geopolitical risk**: Conflict, instability → higher gold (safe haven)
- **Central bank buying**: Net purchases → support; net sales → pressure
- **ETF flows**: Inflows → bullish; outflows → bearish
- **Inflation expectations**: Higher inflation expectations → higher gold

### Secondary Drivers
- Mine supply growth (slow-moving, 1-2% annual change)
- Jewelry demand (price-sensitive, seasonal in India/China)
- Technology demand (small, stable)
- Recycling (responsive to price)

### Key Levels to Watch
- All-in sustaining cost (industry average ~$1,200/oz) provides floor
- Central bank buying accelerates below $1,800/oz historically
- ETF liquidation risk above $2,500/oz

## Copper (Cu)
### Primary Drivers
- **China demand** (50%+ of global consumption): PMI, construction, grid investment
- **Global manufacturing PMI**: Above 50 → bullish; below 50 → bearish
- **Supply disruptions**: Strikes, weather, political events
- **Inventory levels**: LME + Shanghai + COMEX warehouse stocks
- **Energy transition demand**: EVs, renewables, grid modernization

### Secondary Drivers
- Scrap supply (price-responsive)
- Smelter treatment charges (TC/RCs indicate mine supply tightness)
- Substitution (aluminum in some applications at high copper prices)
- Green premium (copper viewed as "green metal")

### Key Levels
- C1 cash cost 90th percentile (~$2.80-3.20/lb) provides ceiling for sustained prices
- LME inventory < 200kt = supply tightness signal
- Forward curve backwardation signals near-term tightness

## Lithium (Li)
### Primary Drivers
- **EV sales growth**: Most important single demand driver
- **Battery chemistry mix**: NMC vs LFP (LFP uses no lithium hydroxide)
- **China spot market**: Sets global price direction
- **New mine supply**: Ramp-up of Australian, Chilean, Argentine projects
- **DLE technology**: Could unlock new brine supply at lower cost

### Secondary Drivers
- Energy storage demand (growing but still small vs. EV)
- Government subsidies for EVs (IRA, EU policies)
- Inventory restocking/destocking by cathode makers
- Recycling (currently <5% of supply but growing)

### Key Levels
- Spodumene marginal cost of production (~$800-1,200/t SC6) provides floor
- Prices above $3,000/t SC6 incentivize massive new supply response
- China inventory levels (weeks of consumption) signal near-term direction

## Nickel (Ni)
### Primary Drivers
- **Indonesian supply growth**: NPI and HPAL ramp-up (dominant new supply)
- **Stainless steel demand**: Still 65% of nickel use
- **Battery demand growth**: Class 1 nickel for batteries
- **Class 1 vs Class 2 divergence**: Battery-grade premium
- **LME inventory and positioning**: Short squeeze risk (2022 precedent)

### Secondary Drivers
- Philippine mine supply (weather-dependent, regulatory risk)
- Russian supply (sanctions risk, logistics)
- Recycling (growing from battery recycling)
- High-pressure acid leaching (HPAL) ramp-up challenges

## Iron Ore (Fe)
### Primary Drivers
- **China steel production**: 50%+ of global production
- **China property sector**: Construction steel demand
- **China infrastructure stimulus**: Government spending
- **Grade premiums**: 65% vs 62% Fe spread indicates market conditions
- **Seaborne supply**: Australian and Brazilian shipments

### Secondary Drivers
- Port inventories in China (above 130Mt = bearish)
- Steel mill margins (negative margins → production cuts)
- Seasonal patterns (restocking before Chinese New Year)
- Decarbonization (long-term shift to higher-grade ores)
