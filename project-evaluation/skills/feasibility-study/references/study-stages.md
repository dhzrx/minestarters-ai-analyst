# Feasibility Study Stages

## Scoping Study (Conceptual Study)
- **Accuracy**: ±35-50%
- **Resource basis**: Inferred resources acceptable
- **Engineering**: Conceptual level, order-of-magnitude costs
- **Purpose**: Determine if project warrants further study
- **Typical duration**: 2-6 months
- **Typical cost**: $200K–$1M

## Pre-Feasibility Study (PFS)
- **Accuracy**: ±25-30%
- **Resource basis**: Indicated resources minimum
- **Engineering**: Preliminary engineering, budget-level costs
- **Purpose**: Justify investment in DFS, support project financing discussions
- **Requirements**: Preliminary mine plan, basic metallurgical testwork, environmental baseline, preliminary layout
- **Typical duration**: 6-18 months
- **Typical cost**: $2M–$10M

## Bankable/Definitive Feasibility Study (BFS/DFS)
- **Accuracy**: ±10-15%
- **Resource basis**: Measured + Indicated resources (reserves must be stated)
- **Engineering**: Detailed engineering (typically 20-30% complete)
- **Purpose**: Support construction decision, secure project financing
- **Requirements**: Detailed mine plan, pilot plant testwork, FEED engineering, full EIA, detailed cost estimates (firm quotes), comprehensive risk register
- **Typical duration**: 12-24 months
- **Typical cost**: $10M–$50M+

## Key Differences Between Stages

| Aspect | Scoping | PFS | BFS/DFS |
|--------|---------|-----|---------|
| Resource confidence | Low | Moderate | High |
| Engineering detail | Conceptual | Preliminary | Detailed |
| Cost accuracy | ±35-50% | ±25-30% | ±10-15% |
| Environmental | Desktop | Baseline study | Full EIA |
| Metallurgical | Literature review | Bench-scale | Pilot plant |
| Contingency | 25-35% | 15-25% | 5-15% |
