# JORC Code 2012 — Key Provisions Summary

## Overview

The Australasian Code for Reporting of Exploration Results, Mineral Resources and Ore Reserves (JORC Code, 2012 Edition) is the professional code of practice that sets minimum standards for public reporting of minerals exploration results, mineral resources, and ore reserves. It is maintained by the Joint Ore Reserves Committee (JORC) and is mandatory for ASX-listed companies.

## Competent Person Requirements

- A **Competent Person** is a minerals industry professional who is a Member or Fellow of the AusIMM, the Australian Institute of Geoscientists (AIG), or a Recognised Professional Organisation (RPO)
- Must have a minimum of **5 years relevant experience** in the style of mineralisation, type of deposit, and activity being reported
- Must consent to the inclusion of the report in the form and context in which it appears
- Must be named in the public report with their qualifications and professional memberships

## Resource Classification Criteria

### Mineral Resources

Resources are classified based on **increasing geological knowledge and confidence**:

#### Inferred Mineral Resource
- Estimated from limited geological evidence and sampling
- Sufficient to imply but **not verify** geological and grade continuity
- Based on information gathered through appropriate techniques from outcrops, trenches, pits, workings, and drill holes that may be limited or of uncertain quality
- **Confidence level**: Low — reasonable to assume but not confirm continuity

#### Indicated Mineral Resource
- Estimated from adequately detailed and reliable geological evidence and sampling
- Sufficient to **assume** geological and grade continuity with reasonable confidence
- Based on information gathered through appropriate techniques from outcrops, trenches, pits, workings, and drill holes with spacing close enough to confirm geological and grade continuity
- **Confidence level**: Reasonable — adequate for mine planning at pre-feasibility level

#### Measured Mineral Resource
- Estimated from detailed and reliable geological evidence and sampling
- Sufficient to **confirm** geological and grade continuity
- Based on detailed and reliable information gathered from closely spaced drill holes or other sampling procedures
- **Confidence level**: High — sufficient for detailed mine planning and final feasibility

### Ore Reserves

Reserves are the economically mineable part of Measured or Indicated Resources, demonstrated by at least a pre-feasibility study:

#### Probable Ore Reserve
- The economically mineable part of an **Indicated** (and in some cases Measured) Mineral Resource
- Includes diluting materials and allowances for losses during mining
- Appropriate assessments (mining, metallurgical, economic, marketing, legal, environmental, social, and governmental factors) have been applied

#### Proved Ore Reserve
- The economically mineable part of a **Measured** Mineral Resource
- Includes diluting materials and allowances for losses during mining
- Highest confidence category — all modifying factors fully applied

## Reporting Requirements

### Table 1 Checklist

All public resource/reserve reports must address the items in JORC Table 1, which covers:

1. **Section 1 — Sampling Techniques and Data**: Drill type, sample recovery, logging, QAQC
2. **Section 2 — Reporting of Exploration Results**: Data aggregation, relationship to mineralisation
3. **Section 3 — Estimation and Reporting of Mineral Resources**: Database integrity, geological interpretation, estimation methodology, classification criteria, cut-off grades
4. **Section 4 — Estimation and Reporting of Ore Reserves**: Mining method, processing, infrastructure, environmental, capital and operating costs

### Key Reporting Principles

- **Transparency**: Reports must contain sufficient information for a reasonable investor
- **Materiality**: Reports must contain all information that might reasonably be expected to influence an investor's decisions
- **Competence**: Reports must be based on work done by a Competent Person

## Material Change Obligations

- Companies must promptly report any **material changes** to previously reported resources or reserves
- Annual review of resources and reserves is expected
- Changes must be accompanied by an explanation of the reasons (new data, price changes, depletion, etc.)

## Relationship to Other Codes

- JORC is a member of the **Committee for Mineral Reserves International Reporting Standards (CRIRSCO)**
- Substantially aligned with NI 43-101 (Canada), SAMREC (South Africa), SME Guide (USA), and other CRIRSCO-template codes
- Cross-recognition of Competent Persons from RPOs in other CRIRSCO jurisdictions
