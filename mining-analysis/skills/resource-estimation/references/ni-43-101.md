# NI 43-101 — Canadian Standard for Mineral Project Disclosure

## Overview

National Instrument 43-101 (NI 43-101) is the Canadian Securities Administrators' (CSA) rule governing the standards of disclosure for mineral projects. It applies to all issuers with mineral projects that file on Canadian stock exchanges (TSX, TSXV) or with Canadian securities regulators. The standard ensures that misleading, erroneous, or fraudulent information relating to mineral properties is not published.

## Qualified Person Requirements

- A **Qualified Person (QP)** is an individual who is an engineer or geoscientist with at least **5 years of experience** relevant to the subject matter of the mineral project and the technical report
- Must be a member in good standing of a professional association (e.g., PGeo, P.Eng. in Canada, or equivalent recognised designation)
- Must be **independent** of the issuer for certain report types (e.g., IPO, major acquisition)
- Must consent to the filing of the technical report
- Can be employed by the issuer or be an external consultant (independence rules apply)

## Resource and Reserve Categories

NI 43-101 adopts the resource and reserve definitions established by the **Canadian Institute of Mining, Metallurgy and Petroleum (CIM)**:

### CIM Resource Definitions (aligned with CRIRSCO)

| Category | Definition | Confidence |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Inferred** | Estimated from limited geological evidence; quantity and grade are uncertain | Low |
| **Indicated** | Estimated with sufficient confidence for mine planning and economic evaluation | Reasonable |
| **Measured** | Estimated with high confidence; supports detailed mine design | High |

### CIM Reserve Definitions

| Category | Derived From | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Probable** | Indicated Resources (or Measured) | Positive pre-feasibility or feasibility study |
| **Proved** | Measured Resources | Positive feasibility study with all modifying factors |

## Technical Report Sections

A NI 43-101 technical report must contain the following sections (Form 43-101F1):

1. **Title Page** — Project name, QP name, effective date
2. **Table of Contents**
3. **Summary** — Key findings and conclusions (stands alone)
4. **Introduction** — Terms of reference, sources of information, personal inspections
5. **Reliance on Other Experts** — Identify reliance on non-QP experts (legal, environmental, tax)
6. **Property Description and Location** — Location, mineral tenure, permits, environmental liabilities
7. **Accessibility, Climate, Local Resources, Infrastructure, and Physiography**
8. **History** — Previous ownership, exploration, resources/reserves, production
9. **Geological Setting and Mineralisation**
10. **Deposit Types**
11. **Exploration** — Work done by or on behalf of the issuer
12. **Drilling** — Type, extent, results, QAQC
13. **Sample Preparation, Analyses, and Security**
14. **Data Verification** — QP's independent verification of data
15. **Mineral Processing and Metallurgical Testing**
16. **Mineral Resource Estimates** — Methods, parameters, classification, cut-off grades
17. **Mineral Reserve Estimates** — Mine plan, dilution, recovery, modifying factors
18. **Mining Methods**
19. **Recovery Methods** — Processing plant design and metallurgy
20. **Project Infrastructure**
21. **Market Studies and Contracts**
22. **Environmental Studies, Permitting, and Social or Community Impact**
23. **Capital and Operating Costs**
24. **Economic Analysis** — Cash flow, NPV, IRR, sensitivity
25. **Adjacent Properties** — Relevant nearby projects (with disclaimers)
26. **Other Relevant Data and Information**
27. **Interpretation and Conclusions**
28. **Recommendations** — Proposed work program and budget

## Filing Requirements

- Technical reports must be filed on **SEDAR+** (System for Electronic Document Analysis and Retrieval)
- Filing required within **45 days** of triggering event (resource estimate, feasibility study, production decision, etc.)
- Annual Information Form (AIF) must reference the current technical report
- Material changes require updated disclosure (news release + potential new report)

### Triggering Events for New Technical Reports

- First-time disclosure of mineral resources or reserves
- Material change in resources or reserves
- Preliminary Economic Assessment (PEA), PFS, or FS
- Production decision (with or without a feasibility study)
- IPO, RTO, or significant acquisition

## Key Restrictions

- **Inferred Resources** may not be used to support economic analysis in a feasibility study (PFS/FS); they may only be included in a Preliminary Economic Assessment (PEA) with prominent disclaimers
- **Historical estimates** must be clearly identified as such and cannot be relied upon without restatement by a current QP
- **Exploration targets** must be expressed as ranges and cannot be treated as resources

## Relationship to JORC

NI 43-101 and the JORC Code are both CRIRSCO-template standards and are substantially aligned:

| Aspect | NI 43-101 | JORC Code 2012 |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Jurisdiction | Canada (TSX, TSXV, CSE) | Australia (ASX) |
| Expert title | Qualified Person (QP) | Competent Person (CP) |
| Experience | 5 years relevant | 5 years relevant |
| Filing system | SEDAR+ | ASX Online |
| Resource categories | CIM definitions | JORC definitions |
| Alignment | CRIRSCO template | CRIRSCO template |
| Report format | Form 43-101F1 (28 sections) | Table 1 checklist (4 sections) |

Cross-listing between ASX and TSX may require dual compliance with both codes.
