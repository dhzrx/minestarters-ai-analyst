# Mining Cost Curve Reference

## Overview

Cost curves rank mining operations from lowest to highest cost of production, providing a visual representation of the global supply landscape for a given commodity. They are essential tools for assessing a project's competitive position, validating financial model assumptions, and understanding commodity price support levels.

## Key Cost Definitions

| Metric | Definition | Includes |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| **C1 Cash Cost** | Direct cash cost of production | Mining, processing, on-site G&A, transport to port/refinery, TC/RC. Excludes royalties, depreciation, sustaining CAPEX. |
| **C2 Cost** | C1 + depreciation and amortisation | Adds non-cash charges |
| **C3 Cost** | C2 + indirect costs | Adds head office G&A, interest |
| **AISC** | All-In Sustaining Cost | C1 + royalties + sustaining CAPEX + G&A + exploration (sustaining). WGC standard for gold. |
| **AIC** | All-In Cost | AISC + growth/expansion CAPEX + non-sustaining exploration |

## Cost Ranges by Commodity

### Gold

| Cost Metric | Range | Median | Notes |
|-------------|-------|--------|-------|
| **AISC** | $900 - $1,400/oz | ~$1,150/oz | World Gold Council methodology |
| **C1 Cash Cost** | $600 - $1,100/oz | ~$850/oz | Excludes sustaining CAPEX and royalties |
| **AIC** | $1,100 - $1,800/oz | ~$1,400/oz | Includes growth capital |

Cost curve position benchmarks:
- **First quartile (Q1)**: < $1,000/oz AISC — low-cost, typically open pit, high grade
- **Second quartile (Q2)**: $1,000 - $1,150/oz — competitive, sustainable operations
- **Third quartile (Q3)**: $1,150 - $1,300/oz — mid-cost, vulnerable if price declines
- **Fourth quartile (Q4)**: > $1,300/oz — high-cost, may be uneconomic at lower prices

### Copper

| Cost Metric | Range | Median | Notes |
|-------------|-------|--------|-------|
| **C1 Cash Cost** | $1.00 - $2.50/lb | ~$1.60/lb | Net of by-product credits (Au, Mo, Ag) |
| **AISC** | $1.50 - $3.50/lb | ~$2.30/lb | Including sustaining CAPEX |
| **C1 (gross, no credits)** | $1.50 - $3.50/lb | ~$2.20/lb | Before by-product credits |

Key factors affecting copper costs:
- **By-product credits**: Au, Mo, Ag credits can reduce C1 by $0.30-1.00/lb
- **Grade decline**: Industry-wide grade decline (~0.6% average) pushes costs higher
- **Open pit vs underground**: Underground typically $0.50-1.00/lb higher
- **Leach (SX-EW) vs concentrate**: Leach operations generally lower cost but limited to oxide ores

## Cost Curve Positioning Analysis

### How to Use Cost Curves for Validation

1. **Locate the project on the global cost curve**: Plot the project's C1 or AISC against the global curve
2. **Assess margin**: Current commodity price minus project cost = operating margin
3. **Determine resilience**: How far can the price fall before the project becomes uneconomic?
4. **Compare to peers**: Is the project competitive with comparable operations?

### Quartile Interpretation

| Quartile | Position | Implication |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| **Q1** (0-25th percentile) | Lowest cost | Profitable through most price cycles; attractive investment |
| **Q2** (25-50th percentile) | Below median | Generally profitable; may face margin pressure in downturns |
| **Q3** (50-75th percentile) | Above median | Vulnerable to price declines; needs high prices to generate strong returns |
| **Q4** (75-100th percentile) | Highest cost | At risk of closure in downturns; questionable long-term viability unless costs reduce |

### Price Support Levels

The cost curve defines natural price support levels:

- **90th percentile cost**: Short-term price floor (highest-cost producers shut in)
- **75th percentile cost**: Moderate support (significant supply curtailment)
- **Marginal cost of new supply**: Long-term price floor (incentive price for new mines)

## Red Flags in Model Assumptions

When validating a model, flag cost assumptions that fall:

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Below Q1 of global cost curve | Verify — may be unrealistically optimistic unless clear technical justification |
| Above Q4 of global cost curve | Challenge viability — project may not be economic |
| Significantly below analogous operations | Request supporting evidence (testwork, contracts, engineering studies) |
| No cost escalation over mine life | Flag — costs typically escalate 2-4% annually |
| OPEX declining year-over-year without explanation | Flag — unusual without productivity improvements or grade increase |
